Focus

In this final week of this course, we look at some principles that lead to high quality dialogic interactions in educational settings. We focus on the principle of ‘cumulative’ dialogue. Cumulative means ongoing dialogue from halaqah session to session over time. All the principles covered in this week’s reading are important. We will therefore have future courses that cover these in more detail. However, we are focusing on the ‘cumulative’ principle in this final week because it is most important for dialogue that develops shakhsiyah. The way we have conceptualised shakhsiyah is not a goal but a journey. Although we aspire to be insan al kamil and emulate the sunnah of the Prophet alayhi salat wa salam, we need to be always aware that maintaining and improving shakhsiyah is an ongoing ‘process’ or dimension of ‘being’ Muslim. This ‘process’ happens in many ways, our concern here is the dialogic nature of this journey. The dialogue is between the educator and learner; the learner and herself; the learner and his immediate and broader community and environment. All of these relationships are ongoing and cumulative dialogue will lead to an understanding that we need to constantly reflect on our shakhsiyah to develop it together.
